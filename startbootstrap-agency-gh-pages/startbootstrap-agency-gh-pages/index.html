Tips For OL-Learners

Better Learning, Better Grades

Practice Time Management

Time Management is one of the Most important skill that you have when taking up Online Classes. Creating a Weekly Schedule you will deisgnate certain time to your asignments, reading, studying and extra-curricular activity. Set reminders for each tasks to complete. Make it as your weekly routine.

Create a Study Space

Set up a dedicated learning environment for studying. By completing your work there repeatedly, you’ll begin to establish a routine. Setting up a regular workspace will also help you to stay organized. When setting up your study space, make sure you Have a high-speed internet connection, have the required books, materials, and software for the course and Have headphones for listening to lectures or discussions. Note: Always determine what type of environment will work best for you.

Eliminate Distraction

Stay away from your Social Media Accounts, Netflix and others. Set Up your moood where you can focus. You can also play music that makes you focus.

Actively Participate

Participate in the course’s online forum to help you better understand course materials and engage with fellow classmates. Read what other students and your professor are saying, if you have a question, ask for clarification. If you do feel yourself falling behind, speak up. Don’t wait until an assignment is almost due to ask questions or report issues. Email your professor and be proactive in asking for help.

Set Goal

Set your target goal for your semester for you to be stay motivated all throughout. If you want to be on DEAN's List you should work harder for you to be a DEAN's List.

Review

Do not review the night before the exam. Do the reading two(2) days before the exam and the day after is you rest, do not pressure your brain too much.

Do's in Online Learning

Things that you should Do

Do Wear Appropriate Clothes

Always be presentable. Put on a nice shirt during the school day and change into comfy clothes afterwards.

Do Check Device Before the Start of Class

Ensure that they all function properly, including your monitor, headphones, and microphone.

Do Log In and Rrrive to the Virtual Class On Time

Several minutes before the scheduled start time be in your seat with all your materials gathered when the teacher starts the class virtually.

Do Use the Mute Function

In a busy virtual classroom, the mute function is your friend. Try staying muted during lectures and while other students are asking questions.

Do Participate as much as Possible

Ask questions, offer answers to class questions when prompted and take notes.

Do Use the Chat Feature Responsibly

Use this space to ask for help, provide a quick answer or give feedback to a classmate’s point.

Do Read the Syllabus Beforehand

For you to be prepared for the course material and discussion, as well as meet any specific deadlines outlined in the syllabus.

Dont's in Online Learning

Thing that you Should Not Do

Do Not Ignore Additional Online Resources

Always pay attention to the Extra or Additional Online resources that the professors gave you.

Do Not Get Discouraged

A positive attitude will help get you through your online classes. Make sure you give yourself breaks. Always Keep Motivated

Don’t Be a Wallflower, Don't Be Afraid to Speak Up

Even in-person classes require students to be active participants. Professors will be expecting you to participate even in an online class.

Don’t Sit Yourself Around Distractions

Study spaces should be away from tempting diversions like the TV, game console or a family pet. Keep you're using laptop for the meeting put away your phone.

Don’t Post Unnecessary Links and Memes in the Chat

The classroom chat is not the place to socialize with your peers. Save funny videos, memes and gifs for private messaging with your friends.

Don’t Snack Without Muting Yourself First.

Nobody wants to hear loud chewing noises. No matter the snack, go on mute before taking a bite.

Google Classroom

Aims to simplify creating, distributing, and grading assignments and engaging students in learning online or remotely. Google Classroom is a free application designed to help students and teachers communicate, collaborate, organize and manage assignments, go paperless, and much more!

Steps in Enrolling to a Class in Google Class

  1. 1. Get the classroom code to your teacher/professor.
  2. 2. Go to classroom.google.com and click Sign In. Sign in with your Google Account.
  3. 3. Make sure to sign in with the correct account.
  4. 4. At the top, click Join class .
  5. 5. Enter the class code from your teacher and click Join.

Google Meet

Businesses, schools, and other organizations can take advantage of advanced features, including meetings with up to 250 internal or external participants and live streaming to up to 100,000 viewers within a domain

Steps in Joining a Meet in Google Meet

  1. 1. Get the meet code to your teacher/professor.
  2. 2. Go to meet.google.com.
  3. 3. Click Use a meeting code.
  4. 4. Enter the code and click Continue.
  5. 5. Click Ask to join.
  6. 6. When someone in the meeting gives you access, you'll join it.

Google Drive

You can use Drive on your computer or mobile device to securely upload files and edit them online. Drive also makes it easy for others to edit and collaborate on files.

Steps in Uploading a File in Google Drive

  1. 1. Login to your Google account.
  2. 2. Type the URL - https://drive.google.com/drive/my-drive in the web browser.
  3. 3. Click New button just under ‘Google Drive.’
  4. 4. Select either File Upload or Folder Upload.
  5. 5. Now, browse and select the desired files/folder.
  6. 6. Wait while the file is getting uploaded.
  7. 7. When the uploading is complete, a confirmation message is displayed.
  8. 8. When the file gets successfully saved on the Google Drive, you can view it there.
